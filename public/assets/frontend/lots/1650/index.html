Arena Breakout is a hardcore looter-shooter for mobile phones, modeled after Escape from Tarkov. In the town of Kamona, after many years of civil war, a lot of weapons, bulletproof vests, and other military trophies remain. You will have to go on a raid alone or in a group of up to four people and try to loot as much as possible. Matches last approximately 30 minutes, but if you get killed, it will end much sooner. You will spawn at a random point on the map and to return to the safe house, you’ll need to find exits, which are marked by flares. The game focuses on realism, so there are no mini-maps or highlighted enemy figures. Characters blend well with the environment, making it difficult to spot a concealed enemy in the grass or a sniper on the roof. You’ll need to rely on sound and carefully observe your surroundings to avoid missing an enemy, since dying means you literally lose everything. To increase your chances of survival, we suggest buying Arena Breakout account with a good inventory. The game has helmets, visors, bulletproof vests, and armored chest rigs that protect the most vulnerable body parts. A direct hit to the head or heart will be fatal if the player is unarmored. Good protection will help you survive a few bullets, and taking medkits along will help you heal, as an injured character moans, which can attract enemies. Weapons, as well as the ammunition, are also important since if an enemy can kill you with a few shots while you cannot do the same to them, you are likely to lose.

Arena Breakout has several maps: Farm Lands, TV Center, Valley, Northridge, Arsenal, etc., but you won't be able to try them all right away. This is because many raid entries will be locked until you level up. If you don't want to keep ending up on the farm, we suggest buying an Arena Breakout account with all maps and modes unlocked. Otherwise, you will have to grind levels for a long time, which is quite difficult, especially if you play solo. The maps throw in both well-equipped players who play as a team and complete newcomers. Therefore, if you want to enjoy victories instead of being sent to the safe house in the first minutes of a match, it's worth buying Arena Breakout account with a good boost and gear.
