Aion Classic brings players back to the first versions of the game, to the days when there were no wing feathers, equipment grade was limited to legendary, and jewelry could not be upgraded. You’ll meet Munin again, get your first wings, and conquer the Black Claw tribe. You’ll teleport every few levels to the capital city to learn new skills, and then look for free space on the panel and empty keys on the keyboard to bind another skill. You’ll also climb mountains, checking every stash trying to find and destroy the enemy kisk summoning stone, so you can complete your quests safely. That’s the things Aion Classic is loved for.

It’s quite difficult to upgrade and, most importantly, to equip a character on classic servers, like many years ago. Enemies from the opposite faction await you during missions, and there is not enough experience for completing the story quests, so you have to farm mobs to get to the level cap. It’s even more complicated with equipment: Aion allows players to transfer only crafting items and items looted from ordinary mobs, it’s impossible to buy other items at the auction. If you want an abyss set that offers a bonus to defense and attack in PvP, then you have to complete the dungeons of fortresses (if they are captured by your faction), keep up with the daily quests, and spend the rest of the time looking for enemies to gain more AP. Weapons are also difficult to get, considering that gladiators, mages, and spiritmasters (one of the most popular classes), need special weapons with enhancements for PvP. And that means dozens or even hundreds of attempts to kill Commander Bakarma to get an extendable spear or to kill Lord Lannok that can drop a grimoire or orb with attack speed.
