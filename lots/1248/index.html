Deep, dark space and a void, separated only by a spaceship. There are only crew members and a murder happens. This is how each game in Among Us begins. Roles are randomly selected and determine whether you’ll be on the dark side that has cookies or will try to save yourself together with your team. Each side has its own path to victory. The goals of Crewmates are to complete all tasks all over the ship, fix temporary problems caused by Sabotages, find all Impostors, and eject them off the map. The goal of the Impostor is to kill the majority of the Crewmates or cause a critical Sabotage that the Crewmates fail to fix. Impostors should kill the victims without witnesses, sneaking through vents. After one of the Crew reports a dead body, the fun begins – a party game Werewolf. And at this stage, the Crew gathers to discuss what they know about the Impostor via text or voice chat. Sure, Impostors will try to prove their innocence and accuse another Crewmate. After discussion and voting, the player with the most votes will be ejected from the ship, and the game will reveal if this player was an Impostor. And then the cycle will repeat when someone reports a dead body. As you already noticed, each game is unique, and to make it even more interesting to play, you can customize armless cartoon astronauts to your taste. The in-game shop has five types of cosmetics: hats, skins, nameplates, visor cosmetics, and pets (so you won’t feel lonely in space). You can also get Cosmicubes that are laid out like a roadmap of branching paths, with each path containing various cosmetics. To get all these perks and play as a nice-looking little astronaut, we recommend that you buy Among Us Stars.
